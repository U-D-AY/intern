Interesting Facts About Supernovae

  • A supernova is a powerful and luminous explosion marking the end of a massive star's life cycle.
  • They occur when a star exhausts its nuclear fuel, leading to a gravitational collapse.
  • Supernovae are key sources of heavy elements in the universe, like iron and gold.
  • Some supernovae are triggered by binary systems when one star accumulates too much material from its companion.
  • The remnants of a supernova can form neutron stars or black holes.
